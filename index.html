What's the latest?

More environmental review ahead for OMF South

Later this year, we are going to publish an Environmental Impact Statement for the Operations and Maintenance Facility South in compliance with the National Environmental Policy Act. The Federal Transit Administration will be the lead federal agency.

We published a Draft EIS under the State Environmental Policy Act (SEPA) in 2021 that evaluated three possible sites for the OMF South facility. After a public comment period (engagement summary, comment summary), the Sound Transit Board identified the South 336th Street site in Federal Way as the preferred alternative in December 2021, with all three sites still to be evaluated in a Final EIS. To align the federal and state processes, we will publish a NEPA Draft / SEPA Supplemental Draft EIS in 2023 that evaluates these same alternatives. A joint NEPA /SEPA Final EIS will follow in 2024.

  • There will be a public comment period for the NEPA Draft / SEPA Supplemental Draft EIS.
  • All public comments that were provided on the SEPA Draft EIS in 2021 are still valid and do not need to be resubmitted.
  • We will respond to all substantive comments from both comment periods in the Final EIS.
  • After the Final EIS is published, the Sound Transit Board will select a project to be built.

Sound Transit is still working to confirm the project schedule because of these changes. We are exploring opportunities to help us stay close to our planned opening date of 2029, but the date for opening may be as late as 2032. We will share an updated project schedule when we have more certainty.

What has changed since the SEPA Draft EIS was published in 2021?

We’ve continued advancing our design and engineering work on the project since the 2021 publication of the SEPA Draft EIS. We’re working now to evaluate the potential environmental impacts of these design refinements, which will be incorporated in the NEPA Draft / SEPA Supplemental Draft EIS.

Project refinements
  • We have added a test track and associated access road to the proposed site layouts for South 336th Street (preferred alternative) and South 344th Street alternatives. The test track would help us prepare new and existing light rail vehicles for service. The Midway Landfill Alternative would not include a test track because of varying grades.
  • We’ve made some updates to the South 336th Street (preferred alternative) site layout in close coordination with the City of Federal Way. Changes include Changes include replacing the functionality of 20th Avenue South, which would be closed to make space for the OMF South facility, by extending 18th Place South between South 340th and 336th streets. (Our earlier site design did not include a replacement street for 20th Avenue South.)
    • The 18th Place South extension would also include a multi-use path.
    • If the Sound Transit Board selects the preferred alternative as the site to be built, Sound Transit would submit a street vacation application to the Federal Way City Council for their approval to make these street changes as part of construction for OMF South. We’ll share more information later this year about this process.

An OMF is where Link light rail trains go for cleaning, storage and maintenance, 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Sound Transit needs four strategically located OMF facilities. We need this new OMF in the South Sound to receive, store and service a larger train fleet to support future light rail extensions to Tacoma and throughout the region. To build this new facility, we need approximately 60-70 acres (for reference, one football field is 1.3 acres) near the Federal Way Link Extension.

What's a test track?

Before a new light rail vehicle can start carrying passengers, we need to test how it performs on the track. Since we have light rail service running on the mainline track during most of the day, we currently do all vehicle testing during the short service window from 1-5 a.m. Building a test track will allow us to test vehicles more quickly to get them ready for service—and we have a lot more cars coming online as we expand the regional light rail system! After analyzing where a test track could be in our system, we determined that locating it with the OMF South was the best fit technically and operationally.

Environmental review

What is an Environmental Impact Statement?

Before Sound Transit builds projects, we study the potential impacts each project alternative may have on the natural and built environment in a document called an Environmental Impact Statement. The EIS helps Sound Transit and our project partners evaluate the possible environmental impacts of each potential site during construction and operation. The EIS compares the effects of each alternative to inform the public, agencies and the Sound Transit Board.

In the OMF South Draft EIS which we prepared in compliance with the Washington State Environmental Policy Act (SEPA) in 2021, we looked at how the Midway Landfill, South 344th Street and South 336th Street site alternatives will each affect air and water quality, historical and cultural resources, property acquisition and land use, economic impacts, hazardous materials, ecosystem resources, cost, schedule and more.

Now, we’re combining our state (SEPA) and federal (NEPA) environmental review processes. We will publish a NEPA Draft / SEPA Supplemental Draft EIS that evaluates these same alternatives this year, followed by a joint NEPA / SEPA Final EIS in 2024.

 

Alternatives

On Dec. 16, 2021, the Sound Transit Board identified the South 336th Street site in Federal Way as the preferred alternative for the location for OMF South. A preferred alternative is a statement of where the agency is leaning based on information available at the time. It's not a final decision.

South 336th Street Alternative (preferred alternative)

  • The South 336th Street Alternative is in Federal Way, between South 336th Street and South 341st Place, and between I-5 and SR 99. The site footprint is approximately 65 acres.
  • This site would require 1.4 miles of connecting light rail track from the Federal Way Link Extension that will be part of the Tacoma Dome Link Extension. The preferred alternative also includes a test track to prepare new vehicles for service. The test track would run parallel and on the east side of the connecting light rail tracks.

South 344th Street Alternative

  • The South 344th Street Alternative is in Federal Way, between South 336th Street and South 344th Street, and between I-5 and 18th Place South. The site footprint is approximately 65 acres.
  • This site would require 1.8 miles of connecting light rail track from the Federal Way Link Extension that will be part of the Tacoma Dome Link Extension. The South 344th Street Alternative also includes a test track to prepare new vehicles for service. The test track would run parallel and on the east side of the connecting light rail tracks.

Midway Landfill Alternative

  • The Midway Landfill Alternative is in Kent, between South 246th Street and South 252nd Street, and between I-5 and SR 99. The site footprint is approximately 68 acres.
  • Due to its location along the Federal Way Link Extension line, the Midway Landfill does not include connecting tracks. This site would not include a test track due to varying grades.

 

Next steps

What comes next for the OMF South?

We will evaluate the South 336th Street Alternative, along with other alternatives, in the NEPA Draft / SEPA Supplemental Draft EIS set to be published later this year. The Final EIS, which will include responses to substantive Draft EIS comments and evaluation of the preferred and other alternatives, is scheduled to be issued in mid-2024. After that, the Board will select a project to be built.

Have you received a property owner letter or phone call from us, or think the project may affect your property? A notification of potential impact does not mean a decision has been made to acquire your property. It does mean there’s a possibility we would need to acquire your property in the future. Final decisions on affected properties and acquisitions won’t occur until after the Final EIS and Sound Transit Board decision. If we find that your property is needed for OMF South, Sound Transit will start official conversations around acquisitions and relocation at that time and after an independent appraisal process.
