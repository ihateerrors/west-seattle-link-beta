Alaska Junction Station

Background

While we are studying multiple station locations as part of our ongoing environmental review, we are advancing design on the Preferred Alternative, which is a tunnel station located under 41st Avenue SW straddling SW Alaska Street. This page provides more detail about the Preferred Alternative. We will continue to study all the station locations analyzed in the Draft EIS as part of the Final EIS, anticipated to be published in 2024. The Sound Transit Board will determine the final project to be built after the Final EIS is published.

  • Interest in stations closer to the Alaska Junction
  • Create a destination that reinforces the character of the neighborhood and supports businesses on California Ave SW
  • Prioritize pedestrian connectivity and comfort with enhanced street crossings, wide sidewalks and pedestrian lighting
  • Complete bike connections to the station
  • Investigate ways to connect the station to the future public park on 40th Ave SW with mid-block pathways
  • Integrate station and new development into the existing neighborhood character
  • Consider how the future light rail would continue to the south
  • Improve the five-way intersection at Fauntleroy Way SW and SW Alaska St so it is easier to cross

Learn the latest

Travel patterns and community priorities have shifted since we last asked the community about what they’d like to see in future station areas in 2019. We want to ensure that our station design work reflects current community needs. That's why we’re asking for your input about how you envision these important community spaces in the future.
